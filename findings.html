Results And Discussion

The results are presented into three: "Natural Language Processing", "Regression on Time Series", and "Categorical Data Encoding". Likewise, the discussions are presented into three: "Natural Language Processing", "Regression on Time Series", and "Categorical Data Encoding"



Natural Language Processing

This section wants to answer the question: "Which implant is most frequently mentioned in tweets?"


The implants identified were "Magnet", "Microchip", "Water", "Toxin", and "Others". This is represented using a Feature Comparison on a bar graph. Microchip is the most mentioned implant with 85 tweet counts.


The implant with the highest frequency of tweets is microchip. Magnet is only the second highest frequently mentioned on tweets. Toxin has the least frequency.


Regression on Time Series

This section wants to answer the question: "When were tweets about implants in COVID-19 vaccines prevalent?"


Data modeling via Linear Regression is performed on three time periods.


There is a significant relationship, p=0.03, between the event of COVID-19 vaccine release and tweet counts. There is no significant relationship, p>0.05, between other events and tweet counts.


Categorical Data Encoding

This section wants to answer the question: "Is there a difference with content type between Filipino and Foreign tweets?"


For this section, visualization of the Feature Correlation between content type and language.


Has moderate positive correlation [>0.3] on Emotional Type and Filipino tweets. Has moderate positive correlation [>0.3] on Rational Type and Foreign Tweet. Transactional has weak correlation [<0.1]

Conclusion

Topic Analysis

Based on the natural language process, our hypothesis for the first research question failed. Instead, microchip is the most frequently mentioned implant. Magnet is not the most frequently mentioned implant but is second to microchip.


Time Series Analysis

Based on the linear regression model, our hypothesis for the second research question passed. The release of the COVID-19 vaccine triggered a surge of tweets. The events outside the period of COVID-19 vaccine release had no significant effect on the change in the spread of tweets.
