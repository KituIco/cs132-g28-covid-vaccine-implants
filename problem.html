Here's the motivation of our project.

The emergence of COVID-19 compelled the world not only with global health outbreak but also with misinformation outbreak. At the height of the pandemic, researchers of Stanford University developed a model to visualize the spread of mis/disinformation, which is absurdly identical to how viruses spread (Nelson et al., 2020). The repeated exposure to mis/disinformation has led individuals to be extremely dubious and anxious. As such, affected individuals doubted the safeness of vaccines and hesitated to get vaccination.

Research Question

(1) What events have triggered the spread of tweets stating that COVID-19 vaccines contain harmful implants?

(2) Which implant is the most frequently mentioned in tweets stating that COVID-19 vaccines contain harmful implants?

Hypothesis

(1) The release of COVID-19 vaccine triggered the spread of mis/disinformation about harmful implants on the vaccines.

(2) Magnet is the most frequently mentioned implant in tweets regarding mis/disinformation about harmful implants on vaccines.

Null Hypothesis

(1) The release of COVID-19 vaccine has no correlation with the spread of mis/disinformation about harmful implants on the vaccines.

(2) No particular implant is frequently mentioned in tweets regarding mis/disinformation about harmful implants on the vaccines.

Action Plan

(1) Analyze the posting time of mis/disinformation tweets about harmful implants on the vaccines.

(2) Analyze the frequency of tweets containing certain phrases associated with the different types of implant.
