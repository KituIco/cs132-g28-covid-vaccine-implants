Here's the motivation of our project.

The emergence of COVID-19 compelled the world not only with global health outbreak but also with misinformation outbreak. At the height of the pandemic, researchers of Stanford University developed a model to visualize the spread of mis/disinformation, which is absurdly identical to how viruses spread (Nelson et al., 2020). The repeated exposure to mis/disinformation has led individuals to be extremely dubious and anxious. As such, affected individuals doubted the safeness of vaccines and hesitated to get vaccination.

Research Question

(1) Which implant is the most frequently mentioned in tweets stating that COVID vaccines contain harmful implants?

(2) What events have triggered the spread of tweets stating that COVID vaccines contain harmful implants?

(3) Is there a difference with the content type between Filipino and Foreign misinformed COVID-19 tweets regarding implants and vaccination?

Hypothesis

(1) Magnet is the most frequently mentioned implant in tweets regarding misinformation about harmful implants on COVID vaccines.

(2) The release of COVID vaccines triggered the spread of misinformation about harmful implants on the vaccines.

(3) There is significant difference with the content type between Philippine-based and Foreign-based misinformed COVID-19 tweets.

Null Hypothesis

(1) No particular implant is frequently mentioned in tweets regarding misinformation about harmful implants on the vaccines.

(2) The release of COVID vaccines has no correlation with the spread of misinformation about harmful implants on the vaccines.

(3) There is no significant difference with the content type between Philippine-based and Foreign-based misinformed COVID-19 tweets.

Action Plan

(1) Analyze the frequency of tweets containing certain phrases associated with the different types of implant.

(2) Analyze the posting time of mis/disinformation tweets about harmful implants on the vaccines.

(3) Separate tweets by location which can either be Filipino or Foreign, Then, analyze the content type frequencies of each location.
